# Acceptable Use Policy

This software — `movement-os` — is released under the Apache License 2.0.
That licence permits broad use. This document records what the Stand Up
South Africa (SASA) movement considers ethical use of the software, and
what it does not. Use outside these limits is not a licence violation
but it is a violation of the movement's principles and, in many cases,
of South African or international law.

## The platform is built for

- **Ingesting and preserving primary-source material** — witness statements,
  court records, government publications, commission transcripts, news
  reporting — about matters of public interest, with clear provenance.
- **Verifying claims** against primary sources before publication.
- **Drafting human-approved artefacts** (posts, letters, briefs, scripts)
  in the movement's voice, always with a named human approver on record.
- **Monitoring** legislative and judicial activity relevant to equal
  protection under South African law.
- **Coordinating** lawful petitions, public letters, noticed peaceful
  gatherings, town halls, and public-interest journalism.

## The platform is not to be used for

- **Impersonation.** Do not use `movement-os` to create accounts,
  personas, or content that pretends to be a real individual or
  organisation that has not consented. No synthetic witnesses. No
  synthetic survivors. No synthetic officials.
- **Astroturfing.** Do not use the platform to simulate organic public
  support, to run coordinated inauthentic behaviour, or to mass-produce
  content intended to appear as many independent voices.
- **Mass persuasion.** The platform is not a campaign-ad factory. It
  exists to document, verify, and publish under named human approval.
  Running it as a persuasion bot farm is a violation of this AUP.
- **Surveillance or profiling** of individuals who are not themselves
  the subject of credible, evidence-backed public-interest reporting.
- **Doxxing.** Do not publish identifying information about any person
  — contributor, witness, target of reporting, or public figure —
  beyond what is strictly necessary for the accountability work and
  what is already a matter of public record.
- **Partisan campaigning.** `movement-os` may not be used to endorse,
  attack, or run campaigns for or against any political party. The
  platform is non-partisan by design.
- **Spam or harassment.** Do not use the platform to send unsolicited
  messages, to harass individuals, or to orchestrate pile-ons.
- **Covert or deceptive influence operations**, including foreign or
  domestic influence campaigns that misrepresent their origin.

## Human-in-the-loop is non-negotiable

Every outbound artefact the platform produces must be approved by a named
human operator before publication. The operator attests, on record, that
they have read the artefact in full and accept responsibility for it.
The audit log preserves this attestation. Any fork or modification of
`movement-os` that removes or bypasses the human-approval gate is outside
the scope of this AUP and should not be called `movement-os` or
distributed as part of the Stand Up South Africa movement.

## Disclosure

Any artefact that was drafted or substantially edited with assistance
from `movement-os` must ship with a visible disclosure ("AI-assisted,
approved by [named operator]") and a link to the audit-log entry. This
is a hard requirement of the project; pull requests that remove the
disclosure mechanism will be rejected.

## Reporting misuse

If you believe `movement-os` is being misused — inside this project or
by a fork — please report it to `conduct@standupsa.org` (to be set up).
We will investigate, document, and act.
