# In memory of Leon Eugene Haarhoff

`movement-os` is dedicated to the memory of **Leon Eugene Haarhoff**
(2 June 1973 – 25 May 1993).

Leon was 19 years old. He was a constable in the South African Police,
serving in the Internal Stability Unit, service number 0450282-5. The
South African War Graves Project records that he died on duty at Phola
Park squatter camp on 25 May 1993, and that he is commemorated at
Pretoria (Zandfontein) Cemetery. Maritzburg College, where Leon was a
schoolboy cross-country runner, still awards the **Leon Haarhoff
Memorial Trophy** to its most promising junior cross-country athlete,
most recently in 2025.

That is the public record. The family's account of how Leon died and of
the accountability that followed differs from it. Closing that gap —
the gap between what the record says, what the family was told, and
what actually happened — is the reason this platform exists.

## Why this dedication sits in the repo

`movement-os` is a civic accountability platform. Its purpose is to
pursue the truth about what happened to people, through lawful and
auditable means, and never to accept "no result" as the end of a case.
Leon is not the only name behind this work, but he is the name that
named it.

The dedication is here, in the source tree, because the engineers who
maintain this code should know — every time they open the repo — who
the work is for. It is for Leon, for his family, and for every South
African family whose official record and lived record do not match.

## What this does not mean

- This is not an accusation against any individual. The platform makes
  public claims only when a named human approves them, the evidence is
  sourced, and the principles-hash check has passed.
- This is not a private grievance project. The engineering, the
  licence (Apache-2.0), and the governance are built to serve any
  family, any case, any operator.
- This is not a promise of conclusive truth. The platform's honest
  goal is **maximum evidential completeness**. Some records are
  destroyed. Some witnesses are gone. Where that is the state of the
  world, the platform is required to say so — clearly, and on the
  record — not to guess.
