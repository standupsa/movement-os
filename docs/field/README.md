# Field-investigator protocol

This directory is the working protocol for the **human field layer** of
`movement-os` — the archive runners, witness documenters, records
officers, and case custodians who carry a case the last mile, where
the digital trail is silent.

These are living documents. They are stubs at v0.1. They will be
revised by people with professional experience in South African
human-rights monitoring and legal practice before any production
rollout. Nothing here is legal advice; operators retain legal
responsibility for how they deploy this protocol.

## Documents

- [`intake-form.md`](./intake-form.md) — structured first contact
  with a family, witness, or complainant.
- [`consent-script.md`](./consent-script.md) — the words said aloud
  before any recording, interview, or publication.
- [`interview-checklist.md`](./interview-checklist.md) — how an
  interview is prepared, conducted, and closed, aligned with the
  OHCHR *Training Manual on Human Rights Monitoring* (Chapter 8) and
  the *Minnesota Protocol on the Investigation of Potentially
  Unlawful Death* (revised 2016) where a death is involved.
- [`affidavit-workflow.md`](./affidavit-workflow.md) — turning a
  statement into a sworn affidavit before a Commissioner of Oaths,
  consistent with the Justices of the Peace and Commissioners of
  Oaths Act 16 of 1963 and its regulations.
- [`archive-runner-workflow.md`](./archive-runner-workflow.md) — the
  in-person workflow for NARSSA / NAAIRS, SAPS records, DoD archives,
  municipal archives, and TRC / SAHA collections.
- [`chain-of-custody.md`](./chain-of-custody.md) — how physical and
  digital evidence is logged, hashed, transferred, and preserved.

## Non-negotiables

Everyone in the field layer agrees to these before taking a case:

- No trespass. No hacking. No bypassing authentication.
- No coercion. No intimidation. No deception of witnesses.
- No impersonation of officials, journalists, or family members.
- No public naming of private individuals without informed,
  recorded consent.
- No payments for testimony. Reasonable reimbursement of travel and
  reasonable subsistence only, logged against the case.
- No publication without a signed `Approval` in the platform and a
  human reviewer's sign-off.

Violations end the field worker's relationship with the movement
and are recorded in the audit log.

## Standards

- OHCHR, *Training Manual on Human Rights Monitoring*, Chapter 8
  (Interviewing).
- UN, *The Minnesota Protocol on the Investigation of Potentially
  Unlawful Death* (revised 2016).
- Promotion of Access to Information Act, 2000 (PAIA).
- Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).
- Justices of the Peace and Commissioners of Oaths Act 16 of 1963.
- National Prosecuting Authority — Office for Witness Protection
  triage, where the witness is at risk.
