# Consent script

Read out loud before any recording, interview, or publication
activity. It is intentionally short, plain, and specific. The field
worker reads it in a language the source is comfortable in. If the
source is not comfortable with the field worker's language, the
meeting is rescheduled with a trusted interpreter — never improvised.

## Script — identity and purpose

> My name is _______. I work with Stand Up South Africa. We help
> families, witnesses, and ordinary people pursue the truth about
> things that happened to them or to people they loved, using lawful
> channels — archives, records requests, courts, and field
> interviews.
>
> I am not the police. I am not a journalist. I am not from any
> political party. I am not offering you money. I am not offering you
> a settlement. I am asking if you would like to tell me what you
> know so that we can try to help.

## Script — what will happen with what you say

> What you tell me today will be recorded in our system. By default,
> your name will be kept confidential. We will not publish your name,
> your address, or your photograph unless you later agree in writing.
>
> Some of what you tell me may help us get a record from a government
> department through an access-to-information request. If that is
> something you would like us to try, I will note that.
>
> Some of what you tell me may eventually be used to publish a
> finding about what happened. If that moment ever comes, we will
> come back to you, show you what we plan to publish, and ask you
> again — for a separate, specific, written consent. You can say
> no. Saying no does not cancel the work we do for you.
>
> You can withdraw your consent at any time. If you do, we will
> stop using your information for any new purpose. Some records
> we are legally required to keep (for example our internal audit
> log) we may not be able to delete, but we will mark your entry
> as withdrawn.

## Script — your rights under South African law

> Under the Protection of Personal Information Act, you have the
> right to know what we hold about you, to ask us to correct it, to
> ask us to stop using it, and to complain to the Information
> Regulator.
>
> Under the Promotion of Access to Information Act, you have the
> right to ask a government or private body for records about
> yourself, and we can help you with that request.
>
> If at any point you feel unsafe because you spoke to me, tell me —
> there is a process we can use to try to help.

## Script — what we will not do

> We will not trespass. We will not hack. We will not threaten
> anyone. We will not impersonate a police officer or a journalist
> or a family member. We will not pay you for your testimony. We
> will not name you publicly without your written permission.

## Ask

> Do you understand all of this? Do you have any questions before
> we begin? Are you willing to continue?

## Record

On the consent form or at the top of the recording:

- Date and time (SAST).
- Language used.
- The source's answer (yes / no / conditional).
- Any conditions the source attached.
- Field worker's name and signature.
- Source's name and signature **or**, if identity is withheld,
  the platform case-reference plus a witnessed mark.

If the answer is `no` or `conditional-not-met`, the meeting ends
here. Nothing recorded. Nothing retained.
