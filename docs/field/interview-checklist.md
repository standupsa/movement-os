# Interview checklist

For field workers who are collecting testimony — witnesses,
family members, former colleagues, officials willing to speak.
Aligned with the OHCHR *Training Manual on Human Rights
Monitoring*, Chapter 8 (Interviewing), and — where the case
involves a potentially unlawful death — the *Minnesota
Protocol on the Investigation of Potentially Unlawful Death*
(revised 2016).

## Before the interview

- [ ] Case brief read. You know the status vocabulary (ADR-0002)
      and what question this interview is actually trying to
      answer.
- [ ] Language. The interview will be held in a language the source
      is comfortable in. If an interpreter is needed, the
      interpreter is pre-briefed on confidentiality and the
      consent script.
- [ ] Location. Private, quiet, the source's choice where possible.
      Never a police station, never a workplace the source fears,
      never a venue that would expose them on arrival.
- [ ] Two-person rule. Where practical, two field workers attend.
      One asks, one records. If that is not possible, document why.
- [ ] Materials: consent form, intake form, recorder (with
      consent), notebook, pen, water, tissues.
- [ ] Safety: exit identified. Case custodian knows you are going.
      Expected finish time agreed. If you don't check in by that
      time, the custodian escalates.

## Opening

- [ ] Introduce yourself. Read the consent script (see
      `consent-script.md`) in full. Do not paraphrase it.
- [ ] Confirm consent. Record the answer on paper **and** (if
      recording is consented) on tape.
- [ ] Ask the source how they would like to be addressed and
      whether they want breaks.

## Body

- [ ] Open-ended first. Let the source tell the story in their
      own words, all the way through, without interruption.
- [ ] Follow-up second. Then ask clarifying questions. One
      question at a time. No leading questions. No compound
      questions.
- [ ] Anchor dates. Wherever a date is given, ask what the source
      was doing that week, what season it was, what political or
      public event was happening — human memory anchors well to
      other events and this helps triangulate later.
- [ ] Record names, service numbers, docket numbers, vehicle
      registrations, addresses in **full** and **verbatim**.
- [ ] Where the source is uncertain, record the uncertainty, do not
      clean it up. "I think it was a Tuesday — could have been
      Wednesday" goes in as said.
- [ ] Trauma awareness. If the source becomes distressed, pause.
      Offer water. Offer to stop. Never push. Do not re-ask
      painful questions out of curiosity — only if necessary for
      the record.
- [ ] Minnesota Protocol cues (death cases): location of the
      deceased when last seen alive, identity of last officials
      to see them, condition of the body if observed, chain of
      officials involved, location and date of post-mortem, who
      collected the body, where it was buried or cremated, what
      records were created.
- [ ] Watch for second-hand material. Mark clearly where the source
      is reporting what they saw themselves vs what someone told
      them.

## Closing

- [ ] Summarise back to the source, in their language. Ask: "Did I
      get that right?" Correct on the record.
- [ ] Ask if there is anything the source wants **not** to be part
      of the record. Honour it.
- [ ] Ask: who else should we speak to? Not for referral hunting
      — for triangulation. Record contact intent and get explicit
      permission before contacting anyone the source named.
- [ ] Agree next steps and a check-in date. Put them on paper.
      Give the source a copy.
- [ ] Remind the source how to withdraw consent.
- [ ] Thank the source. Do not promise an outcome. Do not promise
      a timeline.

## After the interview

- [ ] Upload the recording (if consented) and notes to the platform
      within 24 hours. Apply `Intake` + `lawfulBasis` per the
      consent.
- [ ] Hash the recording; create `ChainOfCustodyEntry`.
- [ ] Write your own observer notes — context, demeanour, apparent
      reliability cues — separate from the source's words, and
      mark them as observer notes.
- [ ] Route to the case custodian for triage.
- [ ] Debrief. If the interview covered difficult material, the
      field worker speaks to a debriefer within 48 hours. This is
      a "family-first" principle commitment, not optional.
- [ ] If the source disclosed an imminent risk to themselves or
      others, the field worker notifies the case custodian
      immediately and follows the platform's safety-escalation
      procedure.

## What never enters the interview

- Payments for testimony.
- Leading questions.
- Promises of specific outcomes.
- Impersonation of any official, journalist, or family member.
- Any coercion, threat, or implied consequence of declining.
- Any recording without explicit, on-the-record consent.
